## Related Tax Information

### **Overview of Illinois Taxes**

Illinois has a **flat state income tax rate**. Unlike many other states with progressive income tax rates, Illinois imposes a uniform rate for all income levels. In addition to the state income tax, residents will also need to account for **federal taxes**, **Social Security**, and **Medicare** taxes, which are deducted from your paycheck.

Here are the main taxes you’ll encounter in Illinois:
- **Illinois State Income Tax**: Illinois has a **flat income tax rate of 4.95%** on your taxable income.
- **Federal Income Tax**: This is based on the IRS tax brackets, and you’ll need to enter your filing status to calculate this.
- **Social Security Tax**: The current rate is **6.2%** for wages up to $160,200 (for 2025).
- **Medicare Tax**: The rate is **1.45%**, with an additional 0.9% tax for incomes over $200,000 (for single filers).
- **Other Deductions**: Depending on your specific situation, you may also have deductions like health insurance, retirement plan contributions, or local taxes.

### **Tax Calculation Method**

Our paycheck calculator automatically calculates your after-tax income by applying the following rules:
- **Illinois State Income Tax**: A flat rate of **4.95%** is applied to your taxable income.

- **Social Security & Medicare Taxes**: These mandatory federal taxes will be deducted from your wages.

## Illinois Income Tax Rates

Illinois uses a **flat state income tax rate**, which means that all taxable income is taxed at the same rate. Here are some key points:
- **Flat Income Tax Rate**: **4.95%** for all income levels.
- Unlike states with progressive tax rates, Illinois does not increase the tax rate based on your income.

Since Illinois does not have progressive tax brackets, tax calculations are much simpler compared to states with multiple income tiers. However, you should still account for federal tax brackets and other deductions when calculating your final take-home pay.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### **1. What is Illinois’ state income tax rate?**

Illinois imposes a **flat income tax rate of 4.95%** on all taxable income, regardless of how much you earn.

### **2. Does Illinois have local taxes?**

While Illinois does not have local income taxes, some cities or municipalities in Illinois may impose sales taxes or other local levies, which can affect your overall finances.

### **3. How do I calculate my after-tax income if I have multiple jobs in Illinois?**

If you have multiple jobs, you’ll need to combine your total income and enter the cumulative amount into the calculator. The tax calculation will factor in your combined income, applying the appropriate tax rates.

### **4. How does the flat tax rate impact my income?**

Since Illinois uses a **flat income tax rate**, all of your taxable income will be taxed at 4.95%, making tax calculations simpler compared to progressive states with varying tax brackets.
