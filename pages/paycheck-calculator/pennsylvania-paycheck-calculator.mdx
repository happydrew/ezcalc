## Related Tax Information

### **Overview of Pennsylvania Taxes**

In Pennsylvania, both residents and non-residents are required to pay state income tax. The state tax is applied at different rates depending on income level, and some local taxes (e.g., city taxes) may also apply. Pennsylvania’s tax system has specific rules based on income brackets, and understanding these rules will help you accurately calculate your after-tax income.

### **Tax Calculation Method**

Our paycheck calculator automatically calculates your after-tax income based on the current federal and state tax rates, Social Security, Medicare, and other deductions. Here are the key tax items considered:
- **Federal Income Tax**: Calculated based on the IRS tax brackets.
- **State Income Tax**: Pennsylvania has a flat state income tax rate.
- **Social Security and Medicare Tax**: Calculated according to the current Social Security and Medicare rates.

### **Exemption and Deduction Rules**

Pennsylvania provides various exemptions and deductions. For instance, low-income families may qualify for specific tax exemptions, and there are also certain tax credits that can reduce taxable income. Understanding these exemptions can help you get a more accurate after-tax income.
