Virginia imposes a **state income tax** with **progressive tax brackets**. This means your tax rate increases as your income increases. In addition to state income taxes, you will also be subject to **federal income tax**, **Social Security**, and **Medicare** taxes. Below is a detailed breakdown of Virginia's tax structure:

- **Virginia State Income Tax**: Virginia uses a **progressive income tax system**, with tax rates ranging from **2% to 5.75%** based on your income bracket.

## Virginia Income Tax Rates

Virginia uses a **progressive income tax system**, with rates ranging from **2% to 5.75%**. The following is a breakdown of Virginia’s **2025** income tax brackets:

#### **2025 Virginia State Income Tax Brackets**

| Taxable Income           | Tax Rate     |
|--------------------------|--------------|
| Up to $3,000 (Single)     | 2%           |
| $3,001 to $5,000          | 3%           |
| $5,001 to $17,000         | 5%           |
| Over $17,000              | 5.75%        |

- **Note**: These rates apply to **single filers**. For married couples filing jointly, the income brackets are generally doubled.

### **How Virginia’s Tax System Works**

Virginia’s **progressive tax system** means that the more you earn, the higher your tax rate will be. For example, the first $3,000 of your income is taxed at 2%, the next $2,000 is taxed at 3%, and so on. The highest tax rate of **5.75%** applies to taxable income over $17,000.
