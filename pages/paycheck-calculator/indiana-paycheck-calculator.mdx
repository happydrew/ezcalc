## Related Tax Information

### **Overview of Indiana Taxes**

Indiana imposes a **flat state income tax rate**, along with **local income taxes** in certain areas. In addition to state and local taxes, you will also need to account for **federal taxes**, **Social Security**, and **Medicare** taxes.

Here’s an overview of the key taxes to consider in Indiana:

- **Indiana State Income Tax**: Indiana has a **flat income tax rate of 3.15%** on taxable income.
- **Local Taxes**: Many counties and cities in Indiana impose local income taxes, which can range from **1% to 3.38%**, depending on your location.
- **Federal Income Tax**: Calculated according to the IRS tax brackets, which vary depending on your filing status and income level.
- **Social Security Tax**: The current rate is **6.2%** on wages up to $160,200 (for 2025).
- **Medicare Tax**: The rate is **1.45%**, with an additional **0.9%** surtax for income over $200,000 (for single filers).
- **Other Deductions**: You may also have deductions like health insurance premiums, retirement plan contributions (e.g., 401(k)), or other employer-provided benefits.

### **Tax Calculation Method**

Our paycheck calculator automatically calculates your after-tax income by considering the following:
- **Indiana State Income Tax**: A flat rate of **3.23%** on your taxable income.
- **Local Taxes**: If you live or work in a city or county with local income taxes, the appropriate local tax rate (typically between **1% and 3.38%**) will be applied.
- **Federal Income Tax**: Calculated based on the IRS tax brackets for the current year.

## Indiana Income Tax Rates

Indiana imposes a **flat income tax rate of 3.23%** on all taxable income. This means that regardless of how much you earn, your state income tax will be calculated at the same rate. However, some areas in Indiana also impose **local income taxes**, which can range from **1% to 3.38%**, depending on your county or city.

In addition to the state and local taxes, you’ll also be subject to **federal income tax** and **FICA taxes** (Social Security and Medicare), which are calculated separately. Our calculator helps you factor all of these taxes to give you an accurate estimate of your take-home pay.
