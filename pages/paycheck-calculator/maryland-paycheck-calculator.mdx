These local taxes, combined with **federal taxes**, **Social Security**, and **Medicare** deductions, all contribute to your total tax burden.

Here are the key taxes in Maryland:
- **Maryland State Income Tax**: Maryland uses a **progressive tax rate** system with rates ranging from **2% to 5.75%**, based on your taxable income.
- **Local Taxes**: Many counties and Baltimore City impose additional local income taxes. These rates range from **2.25% to 3.2%**, depending on where you live or work.

## Maryland State Income Tax Rates

Maryland uses a **progressive state income tax system**, which means that the more you earn, the higher your tax rate. The income tax rates in Maryland are as follows:

- **2%** on income up to $1,000 for single filers, $1,500 for married couples filing jointly.
- **3%** on income between $1,001 and $2,000 for single filers, $1,501 and $3,000 for married couples filing jointly.
- **4%** on income between $2,001 and $3,000 for single filers, $3,001 and $4,000 for married couples filing jointly.
- **4.75%** on income between $3,001 and $100,000 for single filers, $4,001 and $150,000 for married couples filing jointly.
- **5%** on income between $100,001 and $125,000 for single filers, $150,001 and $175,000 for married couples filing jointly.
- **5.25%** on income between $125,001 and $150,000 for single filers, $175,001 and $200,000 for married couples filing jointly.
- **5.75%** on income over $150,000 for single filers, $200,000 for married couples filing jointly.

### **Local Income Taxes in Maryland**

In addition to the state income tax, many counties and Baltimore City impose local income taxes. These taxes range from **2.25% to 3.2%** depending on where you live. Local taxes are calculated on top of the state income tax and are an important factor in determining your total tax liability.
