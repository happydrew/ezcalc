## Related Tax Information

### **Overview of Tennessee Taxes**

Tennessee is one of the few states in the U.S. that does **not impose a state income tax**. This can significantly simplify your paycheck calculations because you won’t have to account for state-level income taxes. However, you are still subject to **federal income tax**, **Social Security**, and **Medicare** taxes, which will be deducted from your paycheck.

Here’s an overview of the key taxes and deductions to consider in Tennessee:

- **No State Income Tax**: Tennessee does not have a state income tax, which means you won’t have to pay taxes on your income at the state level.
- **Federal Income Tax**: Calculated based on the IRS tax brackets, depending on your filing status and income.
- **Social Security Tax**: The current rate is **6.2%** on wages up to $160,200 (for 2025).
- **Medicare Tax**: The rate is **1.45%**, with an additional **0.9%** surtax for income above $200,000 (for single filers).
- **Other Deductions**: You may also have deductions like health insurance premiums, retirement plan contributions (e.g., 401(k)), or other employer-provided benefits.

### **Tax Calculation Method**

Our paycheck calculator automatically calculates your after-tax income by considering the following:
- **Federal Income Tax**: This is calculated using the IRS tax brackets.
- **Social Security and Medicare**: These federal taxes are deducted automatically.
- **Employer Deductions**: If applicable, we’ll include deductions for health insurance, retirement contributions, and other benefits.

Since Tennessee does not have a state income tax, your tax calculation will be simpler compared to other states that have both federal and state income taxes.

## Tennessee’s Tax System

### **No State Income Tax in Tennessee**

Tennessee is one of the few states in the U.S. that does not have a **state income tax**. This means you don’t need to worry about paying state-level taxes on your earnings, making it easier to calculate your after-tax income.

However, Tennessee does impose **sales taxes** on goods and services, which is an important consideration for residents. The **state sales tax rate** is **7%,** with local jurisdictions adding additional sales taxes on top of the state rate.

### **Federal Taxes and Other Deductions**

Although Tennessee does not have a state income tax, residents are still subject to federal income taxes and other mandatory deductions like **Social Security** and **Medicare**. These federal deductions will reduce your take-home pay, so it’s important to account for them when planning your finances.

Your paycheck in Tennessee will include **federal income tax**, **Social Security**, and **Medicare** deductions. Since Tennessee does not have a state income tax, there will be no state tax deductions.
